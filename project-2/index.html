Bill of Rights (Amendments 1-10)

  1. Freedom of religion, speech, press, assembly, and petition
  2. Right to keep and bear arms
  3. Protection from quartering of soldiers
  4. Protection from unreasonable searches and seizures
  5. Rights in criminal cases (due process, no double jeopardy, no self-incrimination)
  6. Right to a fair and speedy trial by jury
  7. Right to trial by jury in civil cases
  8. Protection from excessive bail, fines, and cruel and unusual punishment
  9. Protection of rights not specifically enumerated in the Constitution
  10. Powers not delegated to the federal government are reserved to the states or the people
  1. Source: NCCS - Bill of Rights Visit the National Center for Constitutional Studies for the full Bill of Rights text.
